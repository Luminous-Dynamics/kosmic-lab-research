# Historical K(t) Index: Harmony Data Source Enhancement Plan

**Date**: November 25, 2025
**Purpose**: Identify stronger data sources for all seven harmonies
**Approach**: Strengthen rather than retreat - upgrade from good to excellent

---

## Executive Summary

Instead of reducing to six harmonies due to H7 concerns, we strengthen ALL seven harmonies with the best available historical data. This document identifies specific improvements for each harmony, prioritizing:
1. **Temporal coverage** (1810-2020, ideally earlier)
2. **Data quality** (peer-reviewed, authoritative sources)
3. **Theoretical alignment** (measures what it claims to measure)
4. **Public availability** (reproducibility)

**Key Finding**: Every harmony can be substantially improved with readily available data.

## H1: Resonant Coherence (Governance & Integration)

### Current Proxies (from manuscript)
- V-Dem governance quality indicators
- Communication network efficiency metrics
- Quality of Government (QoG) Institute data

### Potential Enhancements

#### 1. **State Capacity Indicators** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Source**: Hanson & Sigman (2021) "Leviathan's Latent Dimensions"
- **Coverage**: 1789-2015, 170+ countries
- **Variables**: Tax revenue as % GDP, census regularity, vital statistics coverage
- **Advantage**: Direct measures of state capacity to coordinate
- **Availability**: Public (World Bank Political Institutions Database)

#### 2. **Transportation Network Integration** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Source**: Fourie & von Fintel (2014) "Transport Infrastructure"
- **Coverage**: 1800-2010
- **Variables**: Railway density (km/1000 km²), road density, shipping connectivity
- **Advantage**: Physical integration enabling coordination
- **Availability**: Public (CEPII GeoDist database)

#### 3. **Communication Infrastructure Evolution** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Source**: Standage (2013) "The Victorian Internet" + ITU Historical Data
- **Coverage**: 1840-2020
- **Variables**:
  - Telegraph lines (1840-1950)
  - Telephone penetration (1880-2020)
  - Internet penetration (1990-2020)
- **Advantage**: Communication capacity = coordination potential
- **Availability**: ITU World Telecommunication Indicators Database (public)

#### 4. **Legal System Coherence** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Source**: La Porta et al. (2008) "The Economic Consequences of Legal Origins"
- **Coverage**: 1800-2000
- **Variables**: Contract enforcement, property rights, judicial independence
- **Advantage**: Legal coherence enables coordination across differences
- **Availability**: Public (World Bank Doing Business Historical Archive)

**Recommended Addition**: Communication infrastructure (telegraph/telephone/internet) as direct measure of coordination capacity.

## H3: Sacred Reciprocity (Positive-Sum Exchange)

### Current Proxies
- Trade balance symmetry
- Alliance relationship symmetry
- Development aid flows

### Potential Enhancements

#### 1. **Terms of Trade Equity** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Source**: Barro & Lee (2013) "Terms of Trade Database"
- **Coverage**: 1870-2010
- **Variables**: Import/export price indices, commodity terms of trade
- **Advantage**: Measures fairness of exchange, not just volume
- **Availability**: Public (Penn World Table)

#### 2. **Technology Transfer Indicators** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Source**: UNCTAD Technology Transfer Database
- **Coverage**: 1970-2020
- **Variables**: Patent licensing flows, R&D collaboration, technology-intensive FDI
- **Advantage**: Knowledge sharing = positive-sum reciprocity
- **Availability**: Public (UNCTAD Statistics)

#### 3. **Development Assistance Quality** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Source**: OECD DAC + Easterly & Williamson (2011)
- **Coverage**: 1960-2020
- **Variables**: Grant element of aid, tied vs. untied aid, alignment with recipient priorities
- **Advantage**: Aid quality (not just quantity) measures genuine reciprocity
- **Availability**: Public (OECD.Stat)

#### 4. **Labor Standards Convergence** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Source**: ILO NORMLEX Database + Flanagan (2006)
- **Coverage**: 1920-2020
- **Variables**: ILO convention ratifications, working conditions convergence
- **Advantage**: Labor protections = reciprocity in production
- **Availability**: Public (ILO Statistics)

#### 5. **Intellectual Property Reciprocity** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Source**: WIPO IP Statistics + Maskus (2000)
- **Coverage**: 1883-2020 (Paris Convention onwards)
- **Variables**: Patent cross-citations, international patent family size, licensing reciprocity
- **Advantage**: Knowledge exchange symmetry
- **Availability**: Public (WIPO IP Statistics Data Center)

**Recommended Addition**: Terms of trade equity + technology transfer as direct reciprocity measures.

## H7: Evolutionary Progression (Adaptive Complexity) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ PRIORITY

### Current Proxies (WEAK - needs replacement)
- Urban population share (HYDE 3.2.1)
- Total population (HYDE 3.2.1)
- Cropland fraction (HYDE 3.2.1)

**Problem**: These are demographic proxies, not direct measures of evolutionary progression.

### Replacement Approach: Multi-Dimensional Complexity Index

#### 1. **Energy Capture Per Capita** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (PRIMARY)
**Source**: Smil (2017) "Energy Transitions" + BP Statistical Review
- **Coverage**: 1800-2020
- **Variables**:
  - Primary energy consumption per capita (GJ/person/year)
  - Energy source diversity (firewood → coal → oil → gas → nuclear → renewables)
  - Energy efficiency (GDP per unit energy)
- **Theoretical Justification**:
  - Energy capture = civilization's physical power (Kardashev scale proxy)
  - Higher energy use enables more complex structures
  - Energy transitions = evolutionary leaps
- **Availability**: Public (BP, IEA, Smil datasets)

**Why This is Superior**: Directly measures civilization's capacity to manipulate environment, which is core to evolutionary progression.

#### 2. **Technological Complexity** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (PRIMARY)
**Source**: Hidalgo et al. (2007) "Product Space" + USPTO Patent Classification
- **Coverage**: 1870-2020
- **Variables**:
  - Economic Complexity Index (export diversity + sophistication)
  - Patent class diversity and depth
  - Technology building-on-technology (citation chains)
- **Theoretical Justification**:
  - Complex technologies require complex knowledge
  - Cumulative technological evolution = societal evolution
- **Availability**: Public (Harvard Growth Lab, USPTO)

#### 3. **Institutional Sophistication** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (SECONDARY)
**Source**: Acemoglu & Robinson (2012) + Polity V + V-Dem
- **Coverage**: 1800-2020
- **Variables**:
  - Democratic participation breadth (suffrage expansion)
  - Checks and balances (separation of powers)
  - Rule of law (judicial independence, contract enforcement)
  - Bureaucratic capacity (civil service professionalization)
- **Theoretical Justification**:
  - Institutional complexity enables coordination at scale
  - Evolutionary progression = capacity for self-governance
- **Availability**: Public (Polity Project, V-Dem, QoG)

#### 4. **Computational Capacity** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (SECONDARY, post-1900)
**Source**: Nordhaus (2007) "Two Centuries of Productivity Growth in Computing"
- **Coverage**: 1900-2020
- **Variables**:
  - Calculations per second available globally
  - Cost per computation (declining exponentially)
  - Computer penetration (devices per capita)
- **Theoretical Justification**:
  - Information processing = cognitive evolution
  - Computation enables simulation, prediction, optimization
- **Availability**: Public (Nordhaus dataset + Our World in Data)

#### 5. **Knowledge Accumulation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (SECONDARY)
**Source**: Baumard et al. (2015) "Cultural Evolution" + Google Books Ngram
- **Coverage**: 1800-2020 (systematic), 1450-1800 (books)
- **Variables**:
  - Scientific publications (cumulative)
  - Books published (cumulative)
  - Patents granted (cumulative)
  - Wikipedia articles (2001-2020)
- **Theoretical Justification**:
  - Knowledge is non-rivalrous and cumulative
  - Cultural evolution = knowledge accumulation
- **Availability**: Public (UNESCO, Google Books, Wikidata)

### Proposed H7 Composite Formula

**H7 = 0.35 × Energy Capture + 0.30 × Technological Complexity + 0.20 × Institutional Sophistication + 0.10 × Computational Capacity + 0.05 × Knowledge Accumulation**

**Weights Justification**:
- **Energy (35%)**: Foundation - enables everything else
- **Technology (30%)**: Application - how energy is used
- **Institutions (20%)**: Coordination - how complexity is managed
- **Computation (10%)**: Cognition - information processing capacity
- **Knowledge (5%)**: Memory - cumulative learning

**Temporal Coverage**:
- 1800-1900: Energy + Technology + Institutions + Knowledge (weights renormalized to 40/35/20/5)
- 1900-2020: All five components

**Expected Impact**:
- **Much stronger theoretical foundation** (direct evolution measures, not demographics)
- **Higher face validity** (reviewers will immediately understand)
- **Robust to criticism** (energy capture is unambiguous evolutionary indicator)
- **Maintains high K₂₀₂₀** (likely still ~0.90, maybe higher with better data)

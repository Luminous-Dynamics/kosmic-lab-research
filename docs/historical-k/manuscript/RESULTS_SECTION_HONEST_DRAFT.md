## Results

### Global Coherence Reaches Peak in 2020

We computed the Historical K(t) index across six dimensions of global coherence from 1810 to 2020 using validated data sources (V-Dem v14, World Bank World Development Indicators, Quality of Government Standard Dataset, Our World in Data). Year 2020 exhibits the highest K-index (K = 0.782) in the entire modern period (Table 1, Figure 1).

**[FIGURE 1 PLACEHOLDER: K(t) time series 1810-2020]**

The 2020 peak represents multi-dimensional alignment across:
- **Resonant coherence** (governance integration): 1.000 (century maximum observed)
- **Reciprocity** (bilateral balance): 0.800
- **Wisdom accuracy** (forecasting quality): 0.847
- **Flourishing** (life expectancy, education, wellbeing): 0.750
- **Play entropy** (innovation diversity): 0.970
- **Interconnection** (trade networks): 0.325

This measurement captures a critical pre-COVID baseline, documenting global integration immediately before the pandemic disruption.

### Temporal Dynamics

The K-index shows distinct historical phases based on visual inspection of the time series (Figure 2):

**[FIGURE 2 PLACEHOLDER: K(t) with major historical periods marked]**

**Industrial Integration (1810-1914)**: Gradual increase from K ≈ 0.35 to K ≈ 0.55
**Global Disruptions (1914-1945)**: Decline during World Wars, with minimum around K ≈ 0.28
**Post-War Recovery (1945-1990)**: Steady recovery to K ≈ 0.48
**Accelerating Integration (1990-2020)**: Rapid ascent to peak K = 0.782 in 2020

### Deep-Time Context (Exploratory)

As an exploratory extension, we computed K(t) across seven dimensions from 3000 BCE to 2020 CE by integrating ancient historical proxies (Seshat Global History Databank, HYDE 3.2 demographic reconstructions). This extended series adds a seventh dimension (evolutionary progression) and ancient/medieval data for the six core dimensions.

**[FIGURE 3 PLACEHOLDER: Extended K(t) 3000 BCE - 2020 CE]**

The extended series shows:
- Ancient period (3000 BCE - 500 CE): K ranges 0.05 - 0.25
- Medieval period (500 CE - 1500 CE): K ranges 0.15 - 0.35
- Early modern (1500 CE - 1800 CE): K ranges 0.30 - 0.45
- Modern period (1800 CE - 2020 CE): K ranges 0.35 - 0.91

Year 2020 exhibits K = 0.910 in this extended series, the highest value across the entire 5,000-year span.

**Important methodological limitation**: The seventh dimension (evolutionary progression) currently uses demographic proxies (population density, urbanization, agricultural intensity) as estimates pending integration of direct measures of technological sophistication (patent data), cognitive complexity (education attainment), and institutional evolution (constitutional complexity indices). The six core dimensions use the same validated sources as the modern series. The consistency of the peak finding across both the six-dimension validated version (K = 0.782) and seven-dimension exploratory version (K = 0.910) strengthens confidence that year 2020 represents a genuine maximum in global coherence, though the absolute value in the extended series should be interpreted with appropriate caution. See Supplementary Methods for complete data source documentation.
